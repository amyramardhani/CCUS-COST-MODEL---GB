KEY METHODOLOGICAL NOTES
================================================================================

COC Calculation (Equation 1-1):
  COC ($/tonne CO2) = [TOC × CCF + FOM + VOM + PF + PP] / Annual CO2 Captured

Where:
  TOC = Total overnight costs (BEC + EPCC + contingencies + owner's costs)
  CCF = Capital charge factor (annuity factor for 30-year life with WACC)
  FOM = Annual fixed O&M costs
  VOM = Annual variable O&M costs
  PF = Purchased fuel (NG for low purity cases only)
  PP = Purchased power

Capital Cost Hierarchy:
  BEC (Bare Erected Cost): Equipment + facilities + direct/indirect labor
  EPCC (Engineering, Procurement & Construction): BEC + EPC services
  TPC (Total Plant Cost): EPCC + project/process contingencies (20%)
  TOC (Total Overnight Cost): TPC + owner's costs
  TASC (Total As-Spent Cost): TOC + escalation + interest during construction

Retrofit Factors:
  High Purity Cases: RDF = 1.01 (blanket retrofit increase on TPC)
  Low Purity Cases: RDF = 1.05 (on TPC)
  NOTE: These are generic factors; actual retrofit costs are site/technology specific

Operating Labor:
  High Purity: 1 additional operator
  Low Purity: 2.3 additional operators (includes AGR unit & boiler operation)
  Base Rate: $38.50/hour + 30% burden

Maintenance: Scaled from initial capital cost (weighted analysis per component)

Consumables & Waste: Calculated at 100% capacity, then adjusted for 85% capacity factor

CO2 REMOVAL SYSTEMS (Low Purity Cases)
================================================================================

Iron/Steel & Cement: Shell CANSOLV post-combustion capture
  - Amine-based solvent
  - Absorber removes 90-99% of CO2 from flue gas
  - Amine regeneration with steam from NG-fired boiler
  - Selective catalytic reduction (SCR) and FGD removal of NOx/SOx
  - Water wash section removes entrained amine

Refinery Hydrogen: Shell ADIP-Ultra pre-combustion capture
  - Proprietary amine-based solvent
  - Absorber removes 90-99% of CO2 from high-pressure feed gas
  - Flash vessel removes mid-pressure CO2
  - Low-pressure steam regeneration
  - Designed for high-pressure application (Quest facility in Alberta)

IMPORTANT METHODOLOGY CAVEATS
================================================================================

1. Class 4 Estimates (AACE 16R-90): ±30% accuracy range
2. Generic site assumption (Midwestern U.S.): Actual costs vary by location
3. 85% capacity factor assumed: Real plants may operate differently
4. Continuous equipment scaling: Discrete sizes may cost differently
5. NG boiler for AGR only (low purity): If CO2 from NG boiler captured,
   would significantly increase available CO2 due to lower concentration
6. No capture of CO2 from NG boiler combustion (low purity cases)
7. Owner's costs include pre-production & startup (6 months)
8. All cases assume EOR pipeline specifications (2,200 psig)
